CJ's Highmarks Website Redesign

In my free time, I redesigned the website for a restaurant called CJ's Highmarks for learning experiences. I kept a consistent theme of repeating wood panels and pictures, and a small color palette of red, white, and black. I used mainly Photoshop and some Illustrator to create this design, and am in the process of coding it with the program Brackets.

Destination Website

This was a project for my Web Design II class. We had to pick a destination, and then design and code a website, with an about, events, lodging, and dining sections. I chose Vancouver, Canada and tried to stick with a simple color palette of mostly blue, white, and some black. The areas with black rather than white highlights, show what would happen if you would hover over that section. For this project, I mainly worked with Photoshop, Illustrator, and Brackets.

Event Website Redesign

This was a project for my Spring Term Web Design class. For this project we had to choose an event from anywhere in the world, and either create a website for it, or redesign their current site. I chose a rock concert in Columbus, Ohio called Rock on the Range, and redesigned their existing website. I again used a small color palette of red, black and white, and tried to keep the wallpapers consistent. I also went with a grunge feel to match the nature of the concert. I used Illustrator and Photoshop to complete this project.

Vector Illustration

This was the first big project for my Intro to Applications class. We had to find an image of a landscape, and then completely trace and vectorize it using Illustrator, so that was the only program I used to complete this.

Logo Design

This was a logo design project for my Graphic Design class. After choosing a company, mine being Star Greenhouses Inc., we had to create three distinctly different looking logos. I tried to really vary the designs between each logo, and use mostly different colors as well. This project was also done solely in Illustrator.

Photoshop Collage

This project was also done for my Intro to Applications class. We had to randomly select three things from a basket, a place, animal, and color, and then created a collage in Photoshop. My choices were Dallas, Texas, a shark, and the color coral. I started off with the city skyline and then had it transition into a more desert looking scene to match the dry state. Texas is also largely associated with animal skulls, so I replaced the common bull skull with my chosen animal. I then used actual coral to bring in my color and spread it throughout the scene. I also added a few extra Texas elements like the road sign and flag symbol, and a few textures to top it all off. This final project was done using Photoshop and Illustrator.

Book Cover Redesign

This project for my Graphic Design class, had us redesign a book cover. I ended up choosing the book Anthem by Ayn Rand, as I had read it earlier that year. The book focuses on a futuristic dystopian society where individuality no longer exists. Due to the dark theme of the book, I wanted the cover to replicate that theme. Much of the book takes place inside an underground tunnel, so I chose that setting for the front cover. The rediscovery of light is also a big occurrence in the book, so I included lightbulbs on the back cover. This final project was completed using Photoshop, Illustrator, and InDesign.

Alternative Energy Icons

This project is the oldest one to date, and was done while I was still at my career tech center in high school. We were required to partake in Business Professionals of America, so me and a group of friends entered the Web Site Design Team competition, where we had to create a website based around alternative energy solutions. My main job was the creation of all the vector image icons that are shown here, and I also assisted with some of the coding. I used Illustrator for the iconography, and Dreamweaver for the coding.

We came in 2nd place at the State competition with our website.

Non-Profit Flyer

This next project was my final big project for my Intro to Applications class. We had to pick a non-profit organization, and a real or made-up event for it, and then make a flyer for that event. I support animal rights, so I chose to make a flyer for Farm Sanctuary's Walk for Farm Animals event. I chose a lighter theme for this flyer that would potentially appeal for all ages. For this project I was using Photoshop and InDesign.

Black & White Photography

The next pieces were not used for a school project. I visited Hocking Hills with some friends this past fall, and while there shot some pictures. I made some slight changes, and then made them all black and white, because I love black and white photography. These were the final pictures, and the only program I used was Photoshop.

Fantasy, Flora, Fauna Illustration

This project was the first one for my Illustration class. The project was called Fantasy, Flora, Fauna and we had to create a concept involving those three things. I think elephants are really peaceful and cool animals, so I wanted to include one for my piece. The fantasy part was introduced with the civilization added to the back of the elephant, and I have small hints of flora with the designs on the elephant. Everything was hand drawn and then outlined in ink.

Limited Edition Package Design

My last piece was for my Spring Term Limited Edition Packaging class. We had to choose a product, item, or general theme, and create a limited edition package for it. I chose to do mine based on the band Hollywood Undead and their 6 main Cd's. We had to include at least three different elements with the package, so I had the CDs with sleeves, a booklet, and the box it all came in. I chose a dark grunge feel for my overall design to match the feel of the band. For this project, I used Photoshop, Illustrator, and Indesign.

Low Poly Castle (Remote Getaway)

This piece is another free time project, that I created using the program Cinema 4D. I started off with modeling the square land platform, then adding the mountains in the background, then modeled the actual castle as well as small additions like the trees. After all the modeling was complete, I added textures to all the surfaces. After a final render, I took the file into Photoshop and tweaked the colors, and added a colored background plus text. Cinema 4D and Photoshop, were the only 2 programs I used for this project.

Low Poly Gorillaz Island (Windmill Island)

This low poly project was based off of the floating island, inside a music video of a band called Gorillaz. It was another free time project, after a friend gave me the idea for the project. I did all the modeling first, and then added textures to all of the low poly structures. After the file was complete, I corrected colors and added a background and text in Photoshop. I only used two programs for this project again, and they were Cinema 4D and Photoshop.

Low Poly Train (Choo Choo)

This last project was another free time Cinema 4D piece, and I followed the same method I used in previous low poly projects. After all the modeling was finished, and the textures were applied, the file was taken into Photoshop for further edits. Those being, color correction, the addition of a background, and some text. Photoshop and Cinema 4D were again, the only 2 programs used for this project.
